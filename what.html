What is MBTI?

The Myers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI) is a popular personality framework that categorizes people into 16 personality types based on how they perceive the world and make decisions.It's based on Carl Jung's theory of psychological types and categorizes people into 16 distinct personality types.

Personality Preferences

MBTI focuses on identifying individual preferences in how people perceive the world, make decisions, and manage their energy, rather than defining them as fixed personality traits.

Four Dimensions

The MBTI assessment utilizes four dichotomies, or paired preferences, to determine personality types:

  • Extroversion (E) vs. Introversion (I): How individuals prefer to gain energy and interact with the world (externally vs. internally).
  • Sensing (S) vs. Intuition (N): How individuals prefer to gather information (through the senses or intuition).
  • Thinking (T) vs. Feeling (F): How individuals prefer to make decisions (logically or emotionally).
  • Judging (J) vs. Perceiving (P): How individuals prefer to organize their lives and interact with the external world (planned vs. spontaneous).

Four Letters

Each individual's personality type is represented by a four-letter code, one letter for each of the four dimensions (e.g., ENFP, ISTJ).

Self-Report Questionnaire

The MBTI is typically completed through a self-report questionnaire with multiple choice questions.
